\chapter{\label{ch:SSH}Two-dimensional extended Su-Schrieffer-Heeger Model with Superconductivity}
As a first departure into the search for systems exhibiting topological 
superconductivity, we begin with a topologically non-trivial system.
We will explore the effects of fermion pairing terms and of external 
magnetic fields on the system.\\
A canonical topological system is the Su-Schrieffer-Heeger (SSH) model of a 
spinless, one-dimensional dimer lattice of fermions with staggered hopping. 
The model obeys a {\it chiral symmetry}, exhibiting two topological phases. 
In the topologically non-trivial phase, a pair of edge states emerges at 
the boundaries, characterised by a {\it winding number}. 
In the interest of exploring two-dimensional superconductivity and the
surface of three-dimensional superconductors, we study a two-dimensional
extension of the SSH model. Bo-Hung extended the model in a manner which 
gives rise to topological modes. Their idea is to stack SSH models in a 
direct product with a one-dimensional Hamiltonian, known as a Type I 
extension. They refer to more complicated extensions as Type II.\\

\section{\label{sec:SSH} One-dimensional SSH with nearest-neighbour intraorbital hopping}
The simplest extension of the Su-Schrieffer-Heeger model with an additional
intraorbital pairing parameter $t$ is given by the following Hamiltonian
\begin{multline}
    \hat{\mathcal H}_\text{SSH} =  
    \sum_{x=1}^{n_x} \sum_{m\in\{A,B\}} 
    \left[
-\mu \hat c_{x, m}^\dagger \hat c_{x, m}
(-t \hat c_{x, m}^\dagger \hat c_{x+1, m} 
+\text{h.c.})
\right]\\
    +
    \sum_{x=1}^{n_x}
    \left[
    -v \hat c_{x, A}^\dagger \hat c_{x, B}
    -w \hat c_{x, B}^\dagger \hat c_{x+1, A}
    +\text{h.c.}
    \right]
\label{eq:one-dimensional_SSH}
\end{multline}

We investigate the phases of the model in Figure \ref{fig:SSH-one_dim}, and note 
that any nearest-neighbour intraorbital hopping is $t\neq0$ gives rise to 
zero-energy states with density in the bulk. Indeed, the topological edge-states
are a delicate situation. Mapping the SSH model onto a multiorbital model of
superconducting, this shows that topological modes exist in a model in which
the multiorbital-singlet term dominates over the conventional BCS singlet term,
and in which the multiorbital-triplet term is negligible. 
In real materials, one would reasonably expect conventional BCS singlet pairing 
to dominate over multiorbital-singlet, and hence a mapping to the trivial
phase of the SSH model.

\section{\label{sec:multiorbital_chain} Multiorbital tightbinding model, breaking chiral symmetry}
An alternative model which breaks chiral symmetry is a multiorbital tightbinding 
chain with closed boundaries and an interorbital shift,
\begin{multline}
    \hat{\mathcal H}_\text{SSH} =  
    \sum_{x=1}^{n_x} \sum_{m\in\{A,B\}} 
    \left[
        -(-1)^m\mu \hat c_{x, m}^\dagger \hat c_{x, m}
\right]\\
    +
    \sum_{x=1}^{n_x}
    \left[
    -t \hat c_{x, A}^\dagger \hat c_{x, B}
    -t \hat c_{x, B}^\dagger \hat c_{x+1, A}
    +\text{h.c.}
    \right]
\label{eq:multiorbital_chain}
\end{multline}

\createfigure{03-02-multiorbital_chain}{fig:multiorbital_chain}

Although similar to the SSH model, the multiorbital shift model does not exhibit 
an entirely gapped bulk. 
As shown in the previous section, the fully bulk-gapped phase of the 
SSH model is extremely delicate and even the slightest intraorbital nearest-neighbour
perturbation gives rise to density in the bulk.

\section{\label{sec:spin_chain} Spin-chain in an external magnetic field}
We now consider a spin-chain, without orbital terms. Such a model would be 
realisable with an externally applied magnetic field, $m$. 
We suppose there is a spin-flip term, $s$, as well as a nearest-neighbour hopping term, $t$. The model is then described by,
\begin{multline}
    \hat{\mathcal H}_\text{SSH} =  
    \sum_{x=1}^{n_x} 
    \left[
        +m\hat c_{x, \uparrow}^\dagger \hat c_{x, \uparrow}
        -m\hat c_{x, \downarrow}^\dagger \hat c_{x, \downarrow}
\right]\\
    +
    \sum_{x=1}^{n_x}
    \left[
    s\hat c_{x, \uparrow}^\dagger \hat c_{x, \downarrow}
    \right]
    +
    \sum_{x=1}^{n_x}
    \sum_{\sigma\in\{\uparrow,\downarrow\}} 
    \left[
    -t \hat c_{x, \sigma}^\dagger \hat c_{x+1, \sigma}
    +\text{h.c.}
    \right]
\label{eq:spin-chain}
\end{multline}

The advantage of this model is an obvious path to engineering: rather than orbitals, 
a spin-chain in an external magnetic field, which is a one-to-one mapping of the model.

\section{\label{sec:spin-triplet_SSH} Non-unitary spin-triplet Su-Schrieffer-Heeger model}
The non-unitary spin-triplet extension of the Su-Schrieffer-Heeger model is given by
\begin{multline}
    \hat{\mathcal H}_\text{triplet SSH} =  
    \sum_{x=1}^{n_x} \sum_{m\in\{A,B\}} \sum_{\sigma\in\{\uparrow,\downarrow\}}
    \left[
-\mu \hat c_{x, m, \sigma}^\dagger \hat c_{x, m, \sigma}
(-t \hat c_{x, m, \sigma}^\dagger \hat c_{x+1, m, \sigma} 
+\text{h.c.})
\right]\\
    +
    \sum_{x=1}^{n_x}\sum_{\sigma\in\{\uparrow,\downarrow\}}
    \left[
    -v \hat c_{x, A, \sigma}^\dagger \hat c_{x, B, \sigma}
    -w \hat c_{x, B, \sigma}^\dagger \hat c_{x+1, A, \sigma}
    +\text{h.c.}
    \right]
    +
    \sum_{x=1}^{n_x}
    \left[
        -\sqrt{2}\Delta \hat c_{x, A, \uparrow}\hat c_{x, B,\uparrow}
    +\text{h.c.}
    \right]
\label{eq:spin-triplet_SSH}
\end{multline}

The original SSH model is paramterised by vector
\begin{equation}
    \mathbf{h}(k)=(v+w\cos{(k)},w\sin{(k)},0).
\end{equation}
Our extended model is parameterised in the following way
\begin{equation}
    \hat{\mathcal H}_\text{triplet SSH} = 
    \sigma_z\otimes\mathbb{1}_2\otimes\mathbf{h}(k)\cdot\bm{\sigma}
    +\left(
        \sigma_{+}\otimes i(\mathbf{d}\cdot\bm{\sigma})\sigma_y\otimes i\sigma_y
    +\text{h.c.}\right)
\label{eq:spin-triplet_SSH},
\end{equation}
where the first term in the Kronecker product refers to the particle-hole channel,
the second corresponds to spin and the final sector is the orbital channel. 

We remind the reader of the three basic symmetries of SU($4$) matrices. 
The antiunitary symmetries are defined in terms of a complex conjugation operator $\mathbf{k}$
(in real space, so that the $k$-term flips sgn)
\begin{equation}
    \mathbb{k}\hat{\mathcal H}\mathbb{k}=\hat{\mathcal H}^{*}(-k).
\end{equation}
Our model breaks time-reversal symmetry (TRS). The corresonding operator \mathbb{t} 
must satisfy the following equation
\begin{equation}
    \mathbb{t}\hat{\mathcal H}\mathbb{t}^{-1}=-\hat{\mathcal H}^{*}(-k).
\end{equation}
A tensor representation of the operator is
\begin{equation}
    \mathbb{t}\equiv\mathbb{1}_2\otimes i\sigma_y\otimes\mathbb{1}_2\mathbb{k}
\end{equation}
which satisfies
\begin{equation}
    \mathbb{t}^{2}=-1
\end{equation}.

The chiral symmetry $\Eta_z$ must satisfy the constraint
\begin{equation}
    \Eta_z\hat{\mathcal H}\Eta_z^{-1}=-\hat{\mathcal H}^{*}(-k)
\end{equation}
for which we find
\begin{equation}
    \Eta_z=\mathbb{1}_2\otimes\mathbb{1}_2\otimes\sigma_z
\end{equation}
and
\begin{equation}
    \left(\Eta_z\right)^2=+1.
\end{equation}
Our model is a CII or DIII material in the classification of topological matter.
In order to determine which, we must study the particle-hole symmetry operator $\mathbb{P}$, 
in which all terms acquire a sgn flip
\begin{equation}
    \mathbb{P}\hat{\mathcal H}\mathbb{P}^{-1}=-\hat{\mathcal H}^{*}(-k).
\end{equation}
A tensor representation for the corresponding operator is 
\begin{equation}
    \mathbb{P}=\sigma_x\otimes\mathbb{1}_2\otimes\mathbb{1}_2\mathbb{k}
\end{equation}

\begin{equation}
    \mathbf{t}^{2}=-1
\end{equation}.
Thus, our material falls into the symetry class DIII. In dimension $d=1$ or $d=2$,
that is a $\mathbb{Z}_2$ topological number.

The characteristic equation is
\begin{equation}
    |\mathbf{h}\dot\bm{\sigma}-\lambda|
    |\mathbf{h}\dot\bm{\sigma}+\lambda|
    |(\mathbf{h}\dot\bm{\sigma}-\lambda)(\mathbf{h}\dot\bm{\sigma}+\lambda)+\Delta\Delta^\dagger|
\end{equation}

\section{\label{sec:type_I} Type I extension}
\subsection{\label{ssec:single_interchain} Single interchain hopping}
The simplest type I extension is s direct product of an SSH model, with 
hopping parameters v, w, with a tightbinding hamiltonian, with single
interchain hopping parameter t, as depicted in Fig {\color{red}ref}. 
The Hamiltonian is given by
\begin{multline}
    \hat{\mathcal H}_\text{SSH 2D} =  
    \sum_{x=1}^{n_x}\sum_{y=1}^{n_y}\sum_{\sigma\in\{\uparrow,\downarrow\}} \sum_{m\in\{A,B\}} 
    \left[
-\mu \hat c_{x, y, \sigma, m}^\dagger \hat c_{x, y, \sigma, m}
\right]\\
+
    \sum_{x=1}^{n_x}\sum_{y=1}^{n_y} \sum_{\sigma\in\{\uparrow,\downarrow\}}
    \left[
    -v \hat c_{x, y, \sigma, A}^\dagger \hat c_{x, y, \sigma, B}
    -w \hat c_{x, y, \sigma, B}^\dagger \hat c_{x+1, y, \sigma, A}
    +\text{h.c.}
\right]\\
+
\sum_{m\in\{A,B\}}
    \left[
    -t \hat c_{x, y, \sigma, m}^\dagger \hat c_{x, y+1, \sigma, m}
    +\text{h.c.}
\right],
\label{eq:single_type_I}
\end{multline}
where the $P$-boundary is taken to be closed and the $Q$-boundary is open.
